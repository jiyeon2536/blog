**putting his foot in his mouth**  
말실수하다  
to say something embarrassing, inappropriate, or tactless  

> *He was just putting his foot in his mouth when he commented on her age.*  
> *I keep putting my foot in my mouth during meetings.*  

**demoted**  
강등되다  
to be moved to a lower rank, position, or status  

> *He was demoted after the company found serious mistakes in the report.*  
> *She got demoted from manager to assistant manager.*  

**erode**  
침식하다  
to gradually wear away or destroy  

> *The river slowly eroded the rocks over thousands of years.*  
> *Constant criticism can erode a person’s confidence.*  

**retaliated**  
보복했다  
to respond to an attack or harm with revenge  

> *He retaliated after being insulted in front of everyone.*  
> *The country retaliated with economic sanctions.*  

**conducting due diligence**  
실사하다  
to carefully investigate or examine something before making a decision  

> *The company is conducting due diligence before acquiring the startup.*  
> *Investors conducted due diligence on the financial records.*  

**forthright**  
솔직한  
direct and honest in speech or behavior  

> *She was forthright about her mistakes.*  
> *I appreciate how forthright he is during negotiations.*  

**unbeknownst (to someone)**  
모르게  
without someone knowing  

> *Unbeknownst to me, they had already planned a surprise party.*  
> *Unbeknownst to the public, the company was facing serious financial problems.*  

**trumps**  
이기다 / 능가하다  
to defeat or be more important than something else  

> *Experience trumps talent in the long run.*  
> *In an emergency, safety trumps convenience.*

**flubbed**  
망쳤다  
to mess up or make a mistake  

> *He flubbed his lines during the play.*  
> *I totally flubbed the presentation this morning.*  

**immense**  
거대한  
extremely large or great  

> *The building was of immense size.*  
> *She felt immense pressure before the exam.*  

**reservations**  
망설임 / 우려  
feelings of doubt or hesitation  

> *I have some reservations about accepting the job offer.*  
> *She expressed reservations before signing the contract.*  

**ramification**  
결과 / 파장  
a consequence or result, often complex or serious  

> *We need to consider the legal ramifications of this decision.*  
> *The policy change had economic ramifications across the country.*  

**inadequacy**  
부족함  
the state of being not good enough or insufficient  

> *He struggled with feelings of inadequacy at work.*  
> *The report revealed the inadequacy of the current system.*  

**pratfall**  
엉덩방아  
a clumsy fall, especially for comic effect  

> *The comedian’s pratfall had the whole audience laughing.*  
> *He slipped on the ice in a perfect pratfall.*  


**prompt (risk-taking)**  
유발하다  
to cause or encourage something to happen  

> *The new policy may prompt risk-taking among investors.*  
> *His comments prompted her to speak up.*  

**takeaway**  
핵심요점  
the main point or key lesson  

> *The main takeaway from the meeting is that we need better communication.*  
> *My biggest takeaway from the book was the importance of patience.*  

**instigate**  
선동하다 / 부추기다  
to start or provoke something, usually negative  

> *He instigated an argument during the meeting.*  
> *The protest was instigated by a small activist group.*  

**P&L (Profit and Loss)**  
손익계산서  
a financial statement showing revenue, costs, and profit or loss  

> *We need to review the P&L before making any major decisions.*  
> *Her job is to manage the company’s P&L.*  

**workforce**  
노동인력  
the total number of people working in a company, industry, or country  

> *The company is expanding its workforce this year.*  
> *The aging population is shrinking the national workforce.*  

**extensively**  
광범위하게  
to a large degree or in great detail  

> *The topic was extensively discussed during the meeting.*  
> *She has traveled extensively throughout Europe.*  

**goodwill**  
호의 / 신뢰  
friendly or helpful feelings toward someone or an organization  

> *The company built goodwill by donating to local charities.*  
> *There’s a lot of goodwill between the two teams.*  

**ramp up**  
늘리다 / 강화하다  
to increase or accelerate something  

> *The company plans to ramp up production next quarter.*  
> *We need to ramp up our marketing efforts.*
